Cultures In The Philippines

Place Food Description
Pampanga Sisig
- The name comes from the Tagalog word sisigan, which means "to make sour"

Ingredients:
- Pork jowl, ears, belly, chicken liver, calamansi, onions, and chili peppers
Batangas Bulalo
- Soup made by slow-cooking beef shanks and marrow bones

Ingredients:
- Beef, bone marrow, leafy vegetables, potatoes, corn, scallions, and fish sauce
Binondo Pancit
- Stir-fried noodles, vegetables, and often meat

Ingredients:
- Rice/chow mein noodles, cabbage, carrots, green beans, bell peppers, meat/seafood, and soy sauce
Kagayan de Oro Kinilaw
- Raw seafood or meat marinated in vinegar and acidic fruit juices

Ingredients:
- Fish/meat, rice vinegar, garlic, ginger, onion, calamansi/lime, and green mango
Ilocos Pinakbet
- Stew made by simmering vegetables in a fermented seafood broth

Ingredients:
- Eggplant, okra, string beans, squash, bitter melon, bagoong, and shrimp paste
